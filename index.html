Groww Product Teardown

Platform
iOS & Android
Year
2024

A comprehensive product teardown examining how Groww's investing experience impacts their ability to convert browsers into active, recurring investors.

Product Overview

Groww is a digital investment platform that democratizes investing in India by offering a simple way to invest in stocks, mutual funds, ETFs, IPOs, and digital gold. Launched in 2017, Groww targets first-time and retail investors with a mobile-first, user-friendly interface.

Who's It For?

Groww primarily serves India's retail investors â€” people who want to build wealth but are intimidated by traditional brokerage platforms. This includes salaried professionals, first-time investors, college students starting their investment journey, and casual traders who want a simple, no-fuss experience.

Core Value Proposition: As CEO Lalit Keshre put it, the goal is to make investing as easy as shopping on Amazon or Flipkart. Just like you can buy a product in 3 clicks on e-commerce platforms, you should be able to invest in 3 taps. Groww removes traditional barriers (paperwork, high minimums, complex jargon, confusing UX) to make wealth creation accessible to everyone.

Competitors

Groww operates in a competitive fintech landscape:

  • Zerodha: Market leader in discount broking, known for Kite platform with advanced trading tools. Strong with active traders.
  • Paytm Money: Backed by Paytm ecosystem, focuses on simplicity similar to Groww but with integrated payments.
  • ET Money: Strong mutual fund platform with goal-based planning features.
  • Upstox: Discount broker with focus on trading and low-cost brokerage.

Groww's Differentiation: Sits between Zerodha (too complex for beginners) and ET Money (limited to MFs). Offers comprehensive investing (stocks + MFs + gold) with beginner-friendly UX.

How It Works

User Journey:

  1. Onboarding: Users sign up with mobile number, complete KYC (Aadhaar + PAN), and link bank account
  2. Discovery: Browse stocks, mutual funds, or explore curated collections ("Top Gainers," "Most Bought")
  3. Research: View stock/fund details, charts, fundamentals, and expert recommendations
  4. Invest: Place orders for stocks or set up SIPs for mutual funds with a few taps
  5. Track: Monitor portfolio performance, gains/losses, and get personalized insights

Business Model

Groww operates on multiple revenue streams:

  • Brokerage Fees: â‚¹20 per executed order for intraday and F&O trades (equity delivery is free)
  • Mutual Fund Commissions: Trail commission from AMCs (Asset Management Companies) on regular plans, typically 0.5-1% of AUM annually
  • Interest on Unutilized Funds: Revenue from idle cash sitting in user accounts

Unit Economics: Groww's revenue grows with user AUM and trading frequency. This aligns perfectly with getting users to invest regularly (North Star Metric).

User Personas

Based on behavior patterns observed through app exploration and user reviews, Groww serves three primary personas:

  • The First-Timer: New to investing, wants to "start somewhere," needs heavy hand-holding, prefers mutual funds over stocks, risk-averse. Drawn to Groww because traditional platforms feel overwhelming.
  • The Casual Investor: Has some investing experience, builds a mixed portfolio of stocks and MFs, checks app weekly, sets SIPs and forgets. Values simplicity and low friction over advanced features.
  • The Active Trader: Trades frequently (daily/weekly), monitors markets actively, higher engagement and revenue per user. Currently underserved by Groww's beginner-focused feature set.

Note: These personas are based on qualitative analysis of user behavior patterns and app store reviews, not internal Groww data.

North Star Metric

Active Investors Making Regular Contributions

This metric captures Groww's true business value:

  • Revenue alignment: Regular contributors = growing AUM = sustainable revenue
  • User value: Users building wealth consistently = fulfilled value proposition
  • Retention indicator: Regular investing = trust and product stickiness
  • Leading indicator: Predicts long-term LTV better than one-time transactions

User Research & Friction Points

Through app exploration and user behavior analysis, I identified three critical friction points:

Pain Point #1: No Pinning on Explore Homepage

The Problem: When users open Groww, they land on the Explore page (the default homepage). This page shows "Recently Viewed" stocks, but there's no way to pin specific stocks to always appear at the top. Users have to scroll through recently viewed items or search every time to check their priority stocks.

Why This Matters:

  • Active investors care about 3-5 key stocks/funds but may have viewed dozens recently
  • "Recently Viewed" is temporal and unpredictable â€” what you care about isn't always what you viewed last
  • Every app open requires manual navigation to find priority investments
  • Friction adds up: open app â†’ search/scroll â†’ find stock â†’ check price. Should be: open app â†’ see pinned stocks immediately

Impact on North Star: When checking your top investments requires extra steps every single time, users check less frequently â†’ miss important price movements â†’ feel less engaged â†’ reduce active monitoring and contributions.

User Quote: "I care about HDFC Bank and my ELSS fund the most, but I have to search for them every time I open the app. Recently Viewed shows random stocks I looked at yesterday."

Pain Point #2: No Day-Wise P&L Analytics Dashboard

The Problem: Day traders and active traders have no way to see their daily profit/loss summary within Groww. To track performance, they have to:

  1. Go to "Orders" â†’ Check each day's transactions manually
  2. Export or manually note down each transaction
  3. Input data into Google Sheets to calculate daily P&L
  4. Create their own summary dashboard outside Groww

There's no built-in view showing: "Monday: +â‚¹2,500 | Tuesday: -â‚¹800 | Wednesday: +â‚¹1,200"

Why This Matters:

  • Day traders need to track daily performance to understand what's working and adjust strategy
  • Manual tracking is tedious and error-prone â€” creates friction that pushes users to competitors
  • Zerodha, Upstox provide built-in P&L dashboards; Groww doesn't
  • Active traders are Groww's highest-revenue users (â‚¹20 per trade, multiple trades daily)

Impact on North Star: Day traders are the most active contributors (in terms of trading frequency and brokerage revenue). When they can't easily track performance in-app, they either switch to competitors OR reduce trading frequency because manual tracking is exhausting. Either way, Groww loses high-value users.

User Quote: "I trade every day but have to maintain my own Google Sheet to see if I'm profitable this week. Why doesn't Groww just show me this?"

Pain Point #3: No Mutual Fund Overlap Analysis

The Problem: Users investing in multiple mutual funds have no way to see if their funds hold the same underlying stocks. They unknowingly create concentrated portfolios.

Why This Matters:

  • Diversification is core to smart investing â€” overlap defeats the purpose
  • Users think they're diversified across 5 funds but are actually concentrated in 10-15 stocks
  • When those stocks fall, entire portfolio suffers â†’ poor returns â†’ loss of trust

Impact on North Star: Users who unknowingly build concentrated portfolios face higher risk and worse returns. Bad outcomes = churned users who stop contributing.

Feature Recommendations

#1: Pinned Section on Explore Homepage (Quick Win)

Solution: Add a "Pinned Stocks" section at the top of the Explore page (the default landing screen when you open the app), positioned above "Recently Viewed":

  • Users can pin stocks from stock detail pages, search results, or portfolio
  • Pin icon appears next to stock name â€” tap to pin/unpin
  • Pinned stocks appear in dedicated "Pinned" section on Explore page with live price updates
  • This section is the first thing users see when opening the app
  • Limit to 5-8 pins to keep it focused and scannable

Why This Works: Familiar pattern (users understand pinning from email, Chrome tabs, etc.), zero learning curve, immediately reduces friction

Visual Mockup:

Mockup showing "Pinned" section above "Recently Viewed" on Explore tab

Impact: Users open app more frequently because checking their priority investments becomes instant. Higher DAU, higher session frequency, more opportunities to act on price movements.

Effort: Low (1-2 weeks dev time)

#2: MF Overlap Analysis Tool (High Value)

Solution:

  • Portfolio view: "Your 5 funds have 45% overlap" with visualization
  • When adding new fund: "Warning: 60% overlap with existing portfolio"
  • Smart recommendations: "Consider Small Cap fund for diversification"
  • Detailed breakdown of common stocks with weights

Why This Works: Protects users from unknowingly building concentrated portfolios. Few competitors offer this. Builds trust by helping users make smarter investment decisions.

Impact: Better portfolio construction â†’ better returns â†’ higher satisfaction â†’ increased retention.

Effort: Medium (3-4 weeks)

#3: Day-Wise P&L Analytics Dashboard (Strategic Bet)

Solution: Create a "Trading Analytics" section showing:

  • Daily P&L View: Calendar view showing profit/loss for each trading day (green for profit, red for loss)
  • Weekly/Monthly Summary: Aggregate performance over time
  • Win Rate: "7 winning days, 3 losing days this month (70% win rate)"
  • Best/Worst Days: Highlight peaks and troughs
  • Per-Stock Performance: "Your best performer: RELIANCE (+â‚¹12,400 this month)"
  • Export Option: Download detailed CSV for tax filing or personal records

Why This Works: Solves real pain point forcing users to use Google Sheets. Keeps traders engaged within Groww. Data already exists, just needs presentation layer. Competitive parity with Zerodha/Upstox.

Impact: Reduce churn among day traders (high-revenue segment), increase trading frequency as tracking becomes easier.

Effort: Medium-High (4-6 weeks for MVP)

Effort: Medium-High (4-6 weeks) | Impact: Medium-High | Risk: Medium

Final Thoughts

Groww has successfully democratized investing for millions of Indians by making it simple and accessible. However, as the platform matures, there's an opportunity to evolve beyond "simple for beginners" to "powerful yet simple" â€” serving both first-time investors and active traders without compromising the core experience.

These three recommendations address different user segments but share a common thread: they remove friction that prevents users from becoming active, regular investors. Whether it's the casual investor who wants quick access to their watchlist, the mutual fund investor who needs smarter diversification, or the day trader who demands better analytics â€” each friction point resolved is a step toward driving the North Star Metric.

The beauty of these solutions is their feasibility. None require radical product redesigns or massive engineering investments. They're additive improvements that enhance the existing experience while laying groundwork for Groww's next phase: becoming the go-to platform not just for starting to invest, but for actively building wealth.
